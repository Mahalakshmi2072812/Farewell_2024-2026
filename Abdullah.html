To the Dealmaker in the Making

The greatest investors didn't start with capital — they started with conviction. And conviction, Abdullah, is something you've never lacked.

Business will test you in ways no classroom fully prepares you for. You will sit across tables where the stakes feel impossibly high, where the numbers blur and the pressure mounts. In those moments, remember: clarity beats cleverness every time.

Study the markets, yes — but study people more. Every negotiation is a story. Every pitch is a relationship. Every deal is built on trust long before it's signed in ink.

There will be ventures that stall, ideas that the world isn't ready for, and partnerships that don't hold. These aren't failures — they're tuition fees paid to the greatest business school that exists: experience.

So build boldly. Think in decades, not quarters. Stay disciplined when the market is greedy, and stay courageous when it is afraid.

The empire you're imagining? It begins with the next decision you make. Go make it count. 💛

— Written with belief in your future
