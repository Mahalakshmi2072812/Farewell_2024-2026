The Innings of Your Life

Every match begins with a toss — luck plays a role, but preparation decides everything after that. You already know this, Akram.

On the pitch, you read the bowler before the ball is bowled. In business, you read the market before the move is made. That same instinct — that split-second decision between defence and attack — is what separates good players from great ones.

Your cover drive has timing. Your business thinking has vision. Both are rare. Both are yours.

There will be innings where you're on the back foot — prices crash, pitches are difficult, partnerships crack. When that happens, play late. Absorb the pressure. Then unleash.

The best business deals, like the best cricket knocks, are built ball by ball. No slog from ball one. Watch it. Read it. Then hit it clean.

You're not just a cricketer who studies business. You're an Akram — and that combination is a rare playing XI that most people never get to field.

Keep your stance wide. Keep your mind wider. The best innings is the next one.
